How Garage Door Springs Work (And Why They Break)

Garage door springs are what make your garage door feel “light.” Without them, your opener would never lift that heavy slab of metal. There are two main types of springs:

  • Torsion springs: Mounted above the garage door, they twist to create tension.
  • Extension springs: Located on either side of the door, they stretch and contract to lift it.

Each spring is designed to handle a specific number of cycles (one open and one close = one cycle), usually around 10,000 cycles. In a busy household, that might only last 7–10 years. Add in Austin’s extreme heat, humidity, and occasional ice storms, and those springs take a beating.

When a spring breaks, it can create a dangerous situation. The door may slam shut unexpectedly, stop halfway, or become crooked. That’s not just inconvenient—it’s a major safety risk for your family.

Warning Signs You Need a Garage Door Spring Replacement

Wondering if your springs are on their last legs? Here are some red flags to look out for:

  • The door only opens a few inches before stopping
  • Loud snapping noise (like a firecracker) from the garage
  • The door feels extra heavy when trying to lift manually
  • Uneven door movement or visible sagging
  • A visible gap in the spring or a stretched-out look

These symptoms are more than just annoying—they signal that your garage system isn’t working safely. And ignoring them could lead to more damage, or worse, an injury and why it may be time for a spring replacement.

Torsion Springs vs. Extension Springs: Which One Do You Have?

Knowing what type of spring your garage uses is key to understanding the repair process:

  • Torsion springs are mounted horizontally above the garage door and offer smoother, more balanced lifting. They tend to last longer and are generally safer.
  • Extension springs run vertically along the tracks on either side of the door. They’re more common in older homes and can be more dangerous if they fail.

Most modern Austin homes, especially newer builds in places like Cedar Park and Pflugerville, use torsion springs for their reliability. But no matter which type you have, Garage Up Austin can handle both with precision and care.

Here’s why you should avoid DIY spring repair:

  • Incorrect removal can cause the door to slam shut or fall
  • You risk serious injury or damaging other parts of the system
  • Warranties on parts and doors can be voided

Professionals like our techs at Garage Up Austin use special winding bars, safety cables, and precise torque tools to handle the job safely. We’ve seen what can go wrong, and trust us—you don’t want to learn the hard way.
